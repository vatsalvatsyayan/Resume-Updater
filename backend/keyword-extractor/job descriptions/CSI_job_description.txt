Summary

Are you passionate about security, authentication protocols, and building highly secure systems that make a real impact? Join our growing IAM Team at a mission-driven SaaS organization powering community banks across the country. We’re building modern, secure, and scalable identity systems to serve institutions that form the backbone of local economies.

Key Responsibilities


Design, code, test, and debug high-quality software focused on IAM services
Own large-scale projects and product rollouts
Maintain and document program changes in CSI’s core applications
Solve complex programming challenges with creativity and precision
Enforce coding standards and contribute to architectural decisions
Champion Agile, CI/CD, and modern development practices
Mentor junior engineers and foster team growth
Investigate issues and drive thoughtful, secure solutions


Qualifications


Bachelor’s degree in computer science, Engineering, or related field
Advanced degrees or certifications in relevant technologies are a plus
Proficient in C#, .NET Core / .NET 8+ for API and middleware development
Strong experience with OAuth 2.0, OpenID Connect, and SAML
Solid understanding of token-based authentication flows (PKCE, Client Credentials, Refresh Tokens)
Familiar with federated identity providers like Azure AD, Okta, etc.
Deep knowledge of web security best practices (CORS, CSRF, XSS)
Experienced with ASP.NET Core middleware and custom auth handlers
Comfortable in Agile/Scrum environments and CI/CD pipelines
Skilled in Git-based workflows and automated deployments
Bonus: Experience with Duende IdentityServer or IdentityServer4


Technical Stack

Our environment includes C#, .NET Core / .NET 8+, ASP.NET, and REST APIs on the backend. For identity protocols, we use OAuth 2.0, OpenID Connect, and SAML. We integrate with providers like Azure AD and Okta, and leverage Duende IdentityServer for federated identity. Security practices include PKCE, CSRF, CORS, and claims-based identity. Our DevOps workflow is built around Git, CI/CD pipelines, and automated deployments, all within an Agile framework.
