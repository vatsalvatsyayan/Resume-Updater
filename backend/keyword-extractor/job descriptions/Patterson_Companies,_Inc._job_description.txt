About the job

Job Summary

The Software Engineer defines, develops, tests, analyzes, and maintains software applications. This includes being involved in understanding the business impact of the necessary work and creating solutions to realize that impact by researching, designing, documenting, and modifying software specifications throughout the development life cycle.

Essential Functions


Collaborate with customers, stakeholders, developers, and other team members in the conceptualization and development of new or existing software applications
Assist in the definition, development, and documentation of software's objectives, deliverables, and specifications in collaboration with internal users and departments
Help research, identify, analyze, and fulfill requirements of all internal and external users and stakeholders
Create software to enhance functionality and performance of applications
Perform software testing on new and existing programs for the purposes of correcting errors and general debugging
Operate and monitor software for performance, maintenance needs, and ongoing success
Participate in defining software development plans, including scoping, scheduling, and implementation
Deploy software through lower environments upon completion of development, testing, and documentation
May provide product and software support for bugs, issues, and escalated incident tickets from end users to resolve application and software issues
Partner with others in resolving problems with software products or company software systems
Comply with Company and department policies and standards; performs other duties as assigned



Essential Functions: Animal Health - Companion


Assist in reverse engineering PiMS systems to understand basic data flows (SQL queries, API calls).
Develop and test middleware components that support UI-to-backend synchronization.
Use Playwright or browser extensions for basic UI automation and data scraping tasks.
Document technical findings and contribute to internal knowledge bases.
Support integration with Payment Gateway APIs for simple transaction flows.



Additional Responsibilities


In addition to the essential functions listed above, the incumbent may perform the following additional functions.
Participate in regulatory and compliance activities



Minimum Requirements


Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science, Information Systems, a related field or equivalent work experience or
Associate's Degree with work experience in a related field
1 year experience applying training and education



Preferred Requirements


Reverse engineering experience (Win32 APIs, packet inspection, system hooks).
Experience integrating with veterinary, dental, or medical PiMS systems.
Experience with Playwright, Selenium, or browser automation tools



Skills And Abilities


Understanding of software development approaches and best practices
Demonstrated knowledge and understanding of database and operating systems
Demonstrated knowledge in contemporary development approaches, functional, object-oriented, procedural
Demonstrated knowledge of proficiency in at least one modern programming language
Ability to conduct research into software-related issues and products
Knowledge of all aspects of application development and product life cycles, including agile approaches
Companion PAYFAC ONLY: Fluent in at least one modern programming language (C#, Python, or JavaScript).
Companion PAYFAC ONLY: Comfortable reading SQL traces, inspecting HTTP traffic, and automating Windows UI elements.
Companion PAYFAC ONLY: Familiar with payment concepts like tokenization, chargebacks, settlement, merchant onboarding, and 3-DSecure.

Patterson strengthens the oral and animal health markets in North America and the United Kingdom. From small, private practices to large group networks and production operations, we guide our customers with bold solutions and a personal touch. We are much more than a distributor – we are an indispensable partner.

Patterson Dental has strengthened the dental industry since our company’s founding in 1877. Today, Patterson Dental continues to partner with dental practices of all sizes to provide expertise, products, technologies and services needed to grow productive, modern practices, and keep them running smoothly.

No other company supports the animal health market like Patterson. We entered the animal health industry in 2001 with our acquisition of Webster Veterinary and expanded with our 2015 acquisition of Animal Health International, Inc. We provide all the technology, products, expertise and solutions needed to deliver exceptional animal care and drive profitability across the companion, production and equine markets.
