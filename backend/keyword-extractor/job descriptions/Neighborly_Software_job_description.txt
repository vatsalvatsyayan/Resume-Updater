About the job

Who We Are

Neighborly Software is a dynamic and rapidly growing software company dedicated to innovation and excellence. Neighborly Software was built to help communities make a difference in the lives of low-income families and vulnerable populations. Our mission is simple, “Helping Communities, Help People.” We live this every day through our Neighbors and by volunteering serving in our local communities. Headquartered in Atlanta, GA, we offer a cloud-based technology platform for the administration and disbursement of governments funds for programs such as Housing, Economic, Community Development, Disaster Recovery/MIT, Home Energy Rebate Programs, and Housing Choice Voucher programs. Since 2016 we have delivered software solutions to 540+ public and private sector entities across the country, making us the proud market leaders of our industry. We were recently voted as one of the "Best And Brightest Companies to work for". Our office is located in the amazing Atlanta Tech Village.




Software Engineer I (Sustaining Engineering)




We are looking for a Software Engineer I who can assist in the analysis, investigation, and resolution of platform production items to support neighbors (clients), client-success, and client-support teams. This role focuses on maintaining system stability, executing tactical data updates, and learning the architecture of an enterprise SaaS platform.




What You Will Be Doing

Key Responsibilities:

Feature & Code Delivery

Implement well-defined features, fixes, and enhancements following established coding standards and SDLC processes
Participate in code reviews and apply feedback to improve code quality and consistency
Support small automation or tooling enhancements for repetitive tasks

Support, Troubleshooting &Operations

Investigate and resolve assigned support tickets and basic production defects
Perform initial debugging, contribute to RCA documentation for moderate-severity issues, and escalate complex cases appropriately
Participate in on-call rotations and provide timely, clear updates during incident handling

Collaboration & Process Alignment

Work with Product, QA, Security, DevOps, and Client-Facing teams to clarify requirements and support issue investigations
Follow established communication and incident-management processes to ensure consistent, reliable handoffs and responsiveness
Contribute to maintaining documentation related to features, fixes, and operational learnings




Qualifications

BS/MS degree or equivalent in Computer Science, Information Systems, or related field
Proficiency in C#/.NET, React, Azure Services(preferred) or AWS
Exposure to services in Azure (or equivalent in AWS): ADF, API-Management, Service Bus, Power BI, Azure Functions
Experience working with monitoring tools App Insights etc.
Familiarity with Azure DevOps, SDLC Tools like JIRA/Confluence
Ability to debug and fix application issues
